Enemy Generator

an enemy-encounter design tool and framework
Threat level indicates how strong the enemy is. When designing an encounter, depending on the wanted difficulty level, the encounter can be a given a maximum enemy-weight. Then, by looking at the threat level as an indicator for enemy-weight, the encounter can then be filled with enemies until the maximum threat level is reached. Threat level can also be seen as a rough indicator for raw stats and numbers like health, ability power and damage of an enemy. Enemies with high threat levels usually have higher raw stats than lower threat level enemies. Threat level is a scale, enemies can be inbetween the given threat levels of low, medium and high.

The behaviour type of the enemy describes if the enemy will actively goes out of it's way to accomplish it's assigned goal.
Active enemies will actively seek out ways to accomplish their goal, active enemies can also be thought of as proactive enemies. These enemies will make sure to take steps to accomplish their goal. Most enemies in games will be active enemies.

"The Wretched Thug enemy type in Hades will actively seek out to punch Zagreus in the face with their giant mace."

Passive enemies on the other hand are not actively seeking out steps to accomplish their goal. Passive enemies can also be thought of as reactive enemies, they strike when an opportunity arises but won't go out of their comfort zone to do so.

"The Skullomat enemy type in Hades has no offensive or defensive capabilities other than spawning another type of enemy called Numbskulls."

"The Man Eater enemy type in Terraria can't move away from it's spawn location but will attack the player if they are in range of an attack."

Any enemies goal can fit into one of these 3 categories. These goals are similar to the player class roles of tank, dps and support, but for enemies.
Attack-Goal-Enemies are enemies with the main and only goal to attack or damage the player directly or indirectly. This type of enemy is the first thing everybody thinks of when thinking of enemies in games.

"Most of Enter The Gungeons gundead enemies like the bullet kin, seek out to inflict pain to the player."

Disrupt-Goal-Enemies are enemies with the goal of disrupting the player in their intended way of playing the game. Disrupter enemies can be simple, for example tank-style enemies which will put themselves in harms way or complex, setting up traps or shifting terrain to disrupt the player. It is important to mention that all goals can involve attacking or damaging the player, often disrupter enemies will need to backup the pressure they apply with punishment if the player doesn't abide to the rules the enemy set up. E.g.: the player gets damaged. So while still being able to inflict damage to the player, their main goal isn't in damaging the player but disrupting the player to enable other enemies in the encounter to exceed in their own goals.

"Diablo III's Grotesques enemy type is an undead enemy that specialices in having a lot of health and attempting to explode close to the player. This enemy distracts and blocks the player from firing at weaker targets in the backlines as well as forcing the player to reposition when they explode."

"Arrogant Lobsters in Gunfire Reborn are backline tanks that will rain hellfire upon the player, forcing players to push in and focus fire on the enemy instead of killing weaker enemies in closer proximity."

Support-Goal-Enemies are enemies with the goal of supporting other enemies. This can include spreading buffs or heals to other enemies.

"High Temple Priests in Archvale will heal and buff nearby enemies to aid them in battle."

It is important to keep in mind that enemies can work at all kinds of different ranges, regardless of their behaviour and goal. Often when designing enemies, there is a trap to fall into, designing the same enemies over and over again. Tank-type enemies often will be close range and sniper enemies will always be long range, but they don't have to be.

"Nuclear Throne's fittingly named enemy "Assassin" is a close range attacker dealing extremely high damage, effectively being a melee sniper."

"Channelers in Dark Souls are surprisingly tanky backline enemies that suffice multiple roles and goals. They can buff nearby enemies, they can attack at long range with magic and they can also stab the player at close range with their tridents."
